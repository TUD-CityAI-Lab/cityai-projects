    <h4 id="roos-terra">Destination, mode choice and psychological factors in European long-distance holiday travels</h4>
    <p>
      Railways are gradually becoming the EU’s infrastructural focus, aiming to strengthen its economic, social and territorial cohesion and to reduce the climate impact of transportation. Beyond infrastructural investments, the EU is to create a single ticket booking platform to improve the experience and rights of international railway passengers. In 2024, the majority of trips between EU member states were for holiday, leisure, or recreation (74%; Eurostat, 2024). While often omitted in transportation research, psychological factors play a role in mode choice, possibly with even a greater impact on international trips - these involve experiencing unfamiliar destinations, likely with an unknown language. My thesis’s objective is to analyse how mode and destination choices are interrelated with psychological factors (mode-related anxieties & risk-taking), and to assess the influence of the key characteristic of the single ticket booking platform - the right to travel on the next available service if the first leg of the booked journey is delayed. In collaboration with CE Delft, a portfolio choice experiment will be conducted with a survey on mode-related anxieties and recreational risk-taking. The results will be used for creation of a model of destination and mode choices involving these psychological factors for European long-distance holiday travels.
    </p>
