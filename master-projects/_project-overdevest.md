    <h4 id="overdevest">The Impact of Car Reducing Measures on Individual
Travel Behaviour: An Activity-Based Approach</h4>
    <p>
      Limiting space for cars is increasingly used as a strategy to improve urban liveability and sustainability. This thesis, developed in collaboration with TNO and as part of the XCARCITY programme, investigates how restricting car use changes travel behaviour, using the redesign of the Hofplein in Rotterdam as a case study. 
      
      Using the activity-based transport model ActivitySim, the research analyses how individuals respond when space allocated to cars is reduced. It explores whether people shift to alternative modes such as cycling, walking, or public transport, adapt their routes, or forego certain trips altogether.

      The study further examines how these behavioral responses differ across socio- demographic groups, with particular attention to income and age, in order to evaluate potential equity implications such as transport poverty.
