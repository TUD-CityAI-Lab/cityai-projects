    <h4 id="modelling-urban-tree-crown-growth">Rooted in Data: Modelling Urban Tree Crown Growth with City-Scale Data</h4>
    <p>
        Rotterdam is investing in improved underground growing conditions for urban trees, with the aim of increasing tree crown volume and strengthening the city’s green infrastructure. This project investigates whether historical LiDAR point cloud data, OBSURV management records, and aerial imagery can be used to model changes in tree crown volume over time. A key question is whether interventions such as tree planting site improvements can be linked to measurable increases in crown growth. The project may also explore predictive modelling to estimate how tree crowns will develop in the future, supporting visual and evidence-based policy decisions. In addition, the work can contribute to monitoring policy obligations, such as ensuring that Rotterdam’s tree crown cover does not decline and starts increasing from 2030 onwards. The final research focus will be shaped by the available data and the student’s interests, in collaboration with Rotterdam’s Biodiversity programme and Advanced Analytics team. For more information about this project, have a look <a href="https://tud365.sharepoint.com/sites/0000913/Lists/projects/DispForm.aspx?ID=1688&e=Te9Gdm">here</a>.
